# 📋 Project Management  
## Cybersecurity Incident Coordination, Governance & Execution

---

## 📌 Overview
This section focuses on the project management and coordination aspects of the cybersecurity incident response. It demonstrates how structured planning, communication, and governance were used to manage the incident effectively and ensure a coordinated response across stakeholders.

Cybersecurity incident response is not just technical — it requires organized management, clear roles, and structured execution to minimize impact and restore operations efficiently.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Coordinate incident response activities across teams  
- Define roles, responsibilities, and communication protocols  
- Track risks, issues, and dependencies during the incident  
- Ensure structured execution of response actions  
- Maintain alignment between technical and business stakeholders

## ⚙️ Key Project Management Components

### 1. Project Planning & Coordination
- Defined incident response plan and execution strategy  
- Coordinated tasks across technical and business teams  
- Ensured alignment with organizational objectives  

---

### 2. RACI Matrix (Roles & Responsibilities)
- Clearly defined roles (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)  
- Assigned ownership for response tasks  
- Ensured accountability during incident handling  

Incident response teams rely on clearly defined roles to coordinate actions effectively. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

---

### 3. RAID Log (Risk, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies)
- Tracked risks and potential threats  
- Monitored issues during incident response  
- Managed dependencies affecting response execution  

---

### 4. Communication Management
- Established communication channels for stakeholders  
- Provided updates on incident status and progress  
- Ensured transparency across teams  

Effective communication is critical to reduce confusion and improve response coordination.

---

### 5. Change Management
- Documented changes made during incident response  
- Controlled updates to systems and processes  
- Ensured traceability of actions
