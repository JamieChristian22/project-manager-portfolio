# 📋 Requirements Analysis  
## Healthcare Needs Assessment, Functional Requirements & System Design Inputs

---

## 📌 Overview
This section focuses on the requirements analysis phase of the Hospital Patient Scheduling System. It demonstrates how stakeholder needs, business objectives, and operational challenges were translated into clear, structured system requirements.

Requirements analysis is a critical phase in system development, serving as the foundation for design, implementation, and successful project outcomes.  

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Identify and analyze stakeholder needs and expectations  
- Define functional and non-functional system requirements  
- Align system features with healthcare workflows  
- Ensure clarity and completeness of requirements  
- Provide a blueprint for system design and implementation  

---

## 🏗️ Requirements Analysis Approach

The analysis followed a structured business analysis framework:

### 1. Requirements Elicitation
- Gathered requirements from stakeholders:
  - Healthcare staff (doctors, nurses)  
  - Administrative personnel  
  - Patients (end users)  
- Used techniques such as:
  - Interviews  
  - Process observation  
  - Workflow analysis  

Requirements elicitation involves collecting needs from stakeholders to understand what the system must achieve. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

---

### 2. Business Requirements Definition
- Defined high-level goals:
  - Reduce patient wait times  
  - Improve scheduling efficiency  
  - Optimize resource allocation  

Business requirements represent the strategic needs of the organization and guide all subsequent analysis. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

---

### 3. Functional Requirements
Defined system behaviors and features:

- Appointment scheduling and booking  
- Real-time availability tracking  
- Patient record integration  
- Notification and reminder system  

Functional requirements describe what the system should do to meet business needs. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

---

### 4. Non-Functional Requirements
Defined system quality attributes:

- Performance (fast scheduling response time)  
- Reliability (minimal downtime)  
- Usability (user-friendly interface)  
- Security (patient data protection)  

Non-functional requirements define how the system performs and ensure quality standards. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

---

### 5. Requirements Modeling & Documentation
- Created process flow diagrams and models  
- Defined use cases and user scenarios  
- Organized requirements into structured documentation  

Requirements analysis involves organizing, modeling, and validating requirements to ensure clarity and usability. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

---

### 6. Validation & Verification
- Reviewed requirements with stakeholders  
- Ensured completeness, accuracy, and consistency  
- Identified gaps and refined requirements  

Validation ensures that requirements accurately reflect stakeholder needs and project goals. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
